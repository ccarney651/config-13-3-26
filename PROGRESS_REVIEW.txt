GARDEN ROOM CONFIGURATOR — PROGRESS REVIEW
===========================================
Date: March 2026


WHAT HAS BEEN BUILT
-------------------

3D VIEWPORT
The 3D viewer is functional and looks good. Customers can orbit, zoom, and
pan the camera naturally. ACES filmic tone mapping and soft shadows give the
render a photographic quality. Interior view mode fades the near walls and
hides the roof so the inside of the room is clearly readable. A floorplan
top-down view is also available. Dimension arrows on the walls are
interactive and update in real time.

DIMENSION CONTROLS
Width, depth, and height sliders update the 3D model live. Draggable arrows
on the ground plane outside each wall give a more tactile alternative to the
sliders. Wall length labels update correctly as the room is resized.

OPENINGS (DOORS AND WINDOWS)
Doors and windows can be placed on any wall, dragged to any position, and
each carries its own style independently. The system prevents overlapping
placements. A right-click context menu allows moving an opening to a
different wall or deleting it. A reasonable variety of door and window
styles are available.

PARTITION WALLS AND INTERIOR DOORS
Freehand interior partition walls can be drawn on any wall axis, resized by
dragging handles, and given an interior door at any point along their
length. Preset rooms use this same system for their walls.

PRESET ROOMS
Bathroom, bedroom, and office presets can be added to the room. Each drops
in with furniture included, stays within the exterior walls, and moves as a
unit when repositioned. Furniture within a preset room stays attached when
the room moves, and can also be independently repositioned after placement.

FURNITURE
35 furniture items across 8 categories (Living, Dining, Office, Bedroom,
Bathroom, Entertainment, Utility, Misc), all using real 3D models at
accurate real-world scale. Furniture can be dragged to reposition, rotated
via scroll wheel or right-click menu, and shows a wireframe highlight and
glow effect when hovered to indicate it is interactive.

ROOF
Apex and flat roof types both work. Apex pitch is slider-controlled and
updates geometry live. Flat roof tilt angle is similarly adjustable. Gable
rake boards and eave fascia trim render correctly on the apex roof. A
veranda can be added with an adjustable depth slider.

CLADDING
18 curated options covering natural timber (cedar, larch, oak, shiplap,
tongue & groove, loglap), thermowood (charred, charred black), composite
(Strongcore, NeoTimber), metal (anthracite, black), corten steel, stone,
and brick. Per-wall cladding override is supported, so different materials
can be applied to different faces of the building.

INTERIOR FINISHES
Floor textures are all real tileable images: oak, farmhouse oak, walnut,
tiles, polished concrete, rubber, and gym black. Four wall finishes also use
real textures: white plaster, charcoal plaster, birch plywood, and oak
panel. The remaining wall finish options use flat colours.

PRICING ENGINE
Comprehensive live pricing covering: foundation type and area, roof style
and finish, cladding, doors and windows, electrical quantities, bathroom
items, heating, decking, and site services. An admin panel allows any rate
to be overridden without touching code, and individual items can be disabled
to hide them from customers entirely. A full itemised quote modal is
available, and a lead capture form builds a mailto enquiry with the complete
specification included.

UNDO / REDO
Full undo/redo history with a 50-step stack. Toolbar buttons disable
correctly when there is nothing to undo or redo.

SAVE / LOAD / PRESETS
Designs can be saved by name, downloaded as a JSON file, re-uploaded, or
deleted. Four built-in presets (Garden Office, Summer House, Studio/Bar,
Compact Retreat) give customers a useful starting point.

ADMIN PANEL
Rate overrides, item enable/disable, and asset upload all function.
Changes apply immediately on the customer-facing side via localStorage
without a page reload.


WHAT IS OUTSTANDING
-------------------
The following is a comparison against the leading competitor product
(gardenroomplanner.com). Items are roughly ordered by customer impact.

1. KITCHEN TAB — NOT STARTED
   The competitor has a full kitchen configurator with top/bottom/tall/island
   and appliance sub-tabs. Cabinet units are resizable and support finish,
   worktop, and handle customisation. We have nothing for this currently.
   This is the single largest visible gap for a garden room product.

2. DECKING — BASIC ONLY
   We have a simple on/off decking toggle with one material choice. The
   competitor offers 6 shape presets (Square, L-Shape, U-Shape, Triangle,
   Semi-Triangle, Stairs), each individually resizable, with per-object
   balustrade options. This needs significant work.

3. BATHROOM COMPLETION
   The bathroom preset room currently contains only a toilet and a cabinet
   used as a vanity placeholder. A shower and basin are missing because no
   suitable 3D models have been sourced yet. These are the most obvious
   omissions when a customer opens the bathroom preset.

4. ADDITIONAL ROOM TYPES
   The competitor offers Cloakroom and Sauna presets in addition to
   bathroom/bedroom/office. Neither exists in our app yet.

5. ADDITIONAL WALL TYPES
   We only support straight partition walls. The competitor also offers
   mezzanine floors, dwarf walls, and L-shape dwarf walls. These would
   require meaningful geometry work to implement.

6. INTERIOR DOOR VARIETY
   We have one interior door type. The competitor offers interior glass
   windows, interior glass doors, and cavity doors as distinct options.

7. EXTERIOR COLOUR CONTROLS — TOO COARSE
   We have a single frame colour picker that applies to all aluminium
   elements. The competitor has separate colour controls for windows, posts,
   flashing, guttering, and door handles independently.

8. BLINDS — NOT IMPLEMENTED
   The competitor offers optional blinds on every window and door in four
   colours. We have no blinds feature.

9. STRUCTURE TYPES — FREESTANDING ONLY
   The competitor supports Partial Opening and Attached Extension modes
   which significantly alter the building geometry. We only model a
   freestanding structure.

10. ELECTRICAL AND LIGHTING — NO 3D PLACEMENT
    We have quantity counters for sockets, switches, and lights which feed
    into pricing. The competitor has all of these as dynamically placeable
    3D objects on the walls. Retrofitting full 3D placement would be a
    significant piece of work.

11. ROOF FINISH GAPS
    The competitor lists Copper, Corrugated, and Coated Tile roof coverings
    we do not have. Their flat roof also has three sliders (angle, thickness,
    trim height) versus our one, and their pitched roof includes a thickness
    slider.

12. RESIZABLE OBJECTS
    Kitchen units and decking pieces on the competitor can be scaled to
    arbitrary dimensions. All our furniture is fixed-size.

13. MOBILE RESPONSIVENESS
    The layout is desktop-first. Basic touch and scroll work but the
    interface is not optimised for tablet or phone use. Given this is
    intended to be embedded on a customer-facing website, this should
    be addressed before public launch.

14. SAVE TO ACCOUNT / CLOUD
    Currently designs are saved to the browser's local storage only. If a
    customer clears their browser they lose their work. A server-side save
    or shareable link would be needed for a production deployment.

MAIN NOTES
-----
The pricing engine is the most complete part of the project and is in good
shape. The 3D viewer and core room-building tools are functional and
presentable(ish). The main body of outstanding work is on the interior
furnishing and customisation side — kitchen, decking, and the finer
exterior finish controls — which are the areas where the competitor is
most ahead.

Theres still some work to be done on fine-tuning certain aspects of the 3D models, specifically the roofs and the foundation, but its fine for now.

The main things I need are a completely comprehensive list of exact products we offer and their prices so they can be input into the tool, and I mean EVERYTHING. Until I get that list then I will have to keep working off of placeholder models and textures.
